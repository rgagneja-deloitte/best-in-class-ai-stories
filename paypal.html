Problem to proposition

PayPal did not need another AI pilot. It needed a connected operating model.

The starting point

Pressure was coming from the top.

The marketing organization was under a CEO efficiency mandate while working across fragmented tools, agencies, and globally distributed teams. A new transformation leader needed something scalable, credible, and executable.

The positioning move

The sell centered on a connected agentic ecosystem.

Instead of pitching isolated AI pilots, the qual framed an enterprise-ready control layer for marketing. The story combined architecture, governance, and tangible proof so the client could move from curiosity to conviction.

Sales journey

The deal advanced because the team linked vision to proof fast.

Pre-2024

Trusted advisor footing

A strong relationship already existed inside PayPal Marketing, which created enough trust to introduce a bigger transformation thesis.

December 2024

Mandate for change

The CEO efficiency mandate reset priorities and created urgency for a credible AI-enabled operating model, not another abstract strategy deck.

Early 2025

Visioning and alignment

The story points to internal visioning work that framed a connected future state and gave business leaders something concrete to react to.

12-week build

Proof unlocked momentum

A working interface and campaign insights agent created executive momentum and adoption pull because leaders could see the future state in action.

What made it distinctive

Three parts of the PayPal qual worked together.

Connected vision

The qual deliberately moved beyond isolated agents and positioned a reusable, enterprise-ready marketing control layer. That was the strategic leap that made the story feel bigger than a pilot.

Speed to proof

The team delivered a production-ready interface and campaign insights agent in 12 weeks. That pace mattered because executive mandates create urgency, but trust only comes from visible, working solutions.

Ecosystem delivery

The work integrated Customer Marketing and Agency teams with M-Suite and HashedIn offshore developers, and it partnered with Google for delivery. The deck presents this alliance-enabled model as part of the win story.

Delivery journey

The build emphasized architecture, UX, and acceleration in parallel.

Approach

  • Anchor on a clear agentic vision before building individual agents.
  • Run UX design, architecture, and engineering in parallel.
  • Deliver a working MVP quickly to generate executive momentum.
  • Align business sponsors and technical owners early for smoother transition.

Execution details

  • 12-week engagement: 4 weeks of design, 8 weeks of agile build.
  • Initial development occurred in Deloitte's GCP environment to accelerate speed and preserve reusable IP.
  • The code was later ported into the client environment once access was ready.
  • The team was explicit about uncertainty and readiness constraints before locking build timelines.

Lessons and implications

What the PayPal qual says about how enterprise AI is being bought now.

Enterprise buyers want connected ecosystems

The story explicitly contrasts a broader agentic ecosystem against one-off AI pilots or single-agent experiments.

Production-ready proof is now table stakes

Large-scale adoption increasingly depends on showing a working experience early, not asking buyers to trust a roadmap alone.

Executive mandates are accelerating investment

Operating model change is becoming the frame for AI spending, especially when efficiency pressure is tied directly to leadership priorities.

Alliance-enabled delivery matters more

The qual positions the cross-team and partner model as part of the capability, not just an implementation detail.
